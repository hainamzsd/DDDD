## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Purpose

This Data Privacy and Retention Policy governs the collection, processing, storage, and disposal of personal data by the **LocationID Tracker (C06)** mobile application, used by Vietnamese commune police officers for cadastral surveys under the National Location Identification System.

### 1.2 Commitment to Privacy

The Ministry of Public Security and local commune police units are committed to:

- ‚úÖ **Legal Compliance:** Full adherence to Vietnamese data protection laws
- ‚úÖ **Data Minimization:** Collecting only necessary information
- ‚úÖ **Purpose Limitation:** Using data solely for cadastral registration
- ‚úÖ **Security:** Implementing appropriate technical and organizational measures
- ‚úÖ **Transparency:** Informing data subjects about data processing activities
- ‚úÖ **Accountability:** Maintaining audit trails and documentation

### 1.3 Key Privacy Principles

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Lawfulness** | Processing based on legal obligations and public interest |
| **Fairness** | No hidden or deceptive data collection practices |
| **Transparency** | Clear communication about data usage |
| **Purpose Limitation** | Data used only for cadastral registration |
| **Data Minimization** | Only mandatory and necessary fields collected |
| **Accuracy** | Verification procedures to ensure data correctness |
| **Storage Limitation** | 10-year retention, then anonymization |
| **Integrity and Confidentiality** | Encryption, access controls, audit trails |
| **Accountability** | Documented policies and compliance procedures |

## 5. Personal Data Processing

### 5.1 Legal Basis for Processing

Per **Decree 13/2023/Nƒê-CP, Article 5**, personal data processing in the C06 app is lawful under:

#### Primary Legal Bases
1. **Legal Obligation (Article 5.1.b):**
   - **Land Law 2013** mandates cadastral registration
   - **Law on Residence 2020** requires location identification
   - Government agencies must comply with these laws

2. **Public Interest (Article 5.1.c):**
   - Cadastral data collection serves public administration
   - Necessary for land management and urban planning
   - Contributes to national economic development

3. **Official Authority (Article 5.1.d):**
   - Commune police officers act under official duty
   - Authority granted by **Decree 01/2018/Nƒê-CP**
   - Part of lawful government function

#### Consent Not Required
Per **Decree 13/2023/Nƒê-CP, Article 5.2**, explicit consent is **NOT required** when processing is based on legal obligation, public interest, or official authority. However, data subjects must be **informed** of the processing.

## 6. Data Subject Rights

### 6.1 Rights Under Vietnamese Law

Per **Decree 13/2023/Nƒê-CP, Articles 16-20** and **Civil Code 2015, Article 38**, data subjects have the following rights:

#### 6.1.1 Right to Information (Article 16)

**What:** Data subjects must be informed about data processing activities.

**Implementation in C06 App:**
- ‚úÖ Officers inform property owners verbally during survey
- ‚úÖ Data Privacy Notice displayed in app (future enhancement)
- ‚úÖ This policy document available on Ministry of Public Security website

**Information Provided:**
1. Identity of data controller (Ministry of Public Security)
2. Purpose of data collection (cadastral registration)
3. Legal basis (Land Law 2013, Law on Residence 2020)
4. Data retention period (10 years)
5. Rights to access, correction, deletion

#### 6.1.2 Right to Access (Article 17)

**What:** Data subjects can request a copy of their personal data.

**Implementation:**
- **Request Method:** Written request to commune police station or email to DPO
- **Response Time:** Within 30 days (Decree 13/2023, Article 17.2)
- **Format:** PDF report with survey details, photos, GPS coordinates
- **Fee:** Free for first request, reasonable fee for subsequent requests

**Access Request Form:** See Appendix A

**Technical Implementation (Future Enhancement):**
- "Request My Data" button in Settings screen
- Automated export in PDF format
- Secure delivery via encrypted email or in-person pickup

#### 6.1.3 Right to Correction (Article 18)

**What:** Data subjects can request correction of inaccurate personal data.

**Implementation:**
- **Request Method:** Written request with supporting documentation (e.g., updated ID card)
- **Verification:** Officer verifies documentation before making changes
- **Response Time:** Within 15 days (Decree 13/2023, Article 18.2)
- **Audit Trail:** All corrections logged with timestamp and reason

**Correction Request Form:** See Appendix B

**Technical Implementation:**
- Corrections made via web dashboard by authorized personnel
- Email notification sent to requester upon completion
- Original data retained in audit log (not deleted)

#### 6.1.4 Right to Deletion (Article 19)

**What:** Data subjects can request deletion of their personal data.

**Implementation:**
- **Request Method:** Written request to commune police station or DPO
- **Limitations:** Deletion NOT permitted when:
  - Data required for **legal obligations** (10-year cadastral retention)

  - Data required for **legal claims** (ownership disputes)

## 8. Data Retention Policy

### 8.1 Legal Retention Periods

Per **Law on Archives 2011, Decree 43/2014/Nƒê-CP, and Cybersecurity Law 2018**:

| Data Type | Retention Period | Legal Basis | Disposal Method |
|-----------|------------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Cadastral Survey Data** | **10 years** | Decree 43/2014, Article 10 | Archive transfer or anonymization |
| **Owner Personal Data** | **10 years** | Law on Archives 2011, Schedule A | Anonymization (remove name/ID, keep coordinates) |
| **GPS Coordinates** | **Permanent** | Decree 43/2014, Article 3 | N/A (historical record) |
| **Survey Photos** | **10 years** | Circular 02/2015, Article 9 | Secure deletion (overwrite + verify) |
| **Audit Logs** | **5 years** | Cybersecurity Law 2018, Article 26 | Secure deletion |
| **Police Officer Data** | **Duration of employment + 5 years** | Civil Service Law 2015, Article 42 | Anonymization |

**Retention Period Start Date:** Date of survey submission (not creation date)

### 8.2 Retention Implementation

#### Automated Retention Tracking

```sql
-- Example retention check query
SELECT id, location_identifier, created_at,
       CURRENT_DATE - DATE(created_at) AS days_old,
       CASE
         WHEN CURRENT_DATE - DATE(created_at) > 3650 THEN 'Due for archival'
         WHEN CURRENT_DATE - DATE(created_at) > 3285 THEN 'Archival in 1 year'
         ELSE 'Within retention period'
       END AS retention_status
FROM survey_locations
WHERE created_at < CURRENT_DATE - INTERVAL '9 years';
```

**Automated Jobs (Future Implementation):**
1. **Daily:** Identify records approaching retention limit (within 6 months)
2. **Weekly:** Generate retention report for compliance officer
3. **Monthly:** Flag records due for archival (10+ years old)
4. **Quarterly:** Execute archival and anonymization procedures

#### Archival Procedures

Per **Circular 07/2012/TT-BNV** (State Archives Regulations):

**Phase 1: Selection (Month 1-2 of new year)**
1. Query all survey records created 10+ years ago
2. Verify data completeness (no missing fields)
3. Generate archival list with record counts

**Phase 2: Export (Month 2-3)**
1. Export selected records to JSON format
2. Include all related data (photos, polygons, metadata)
3. Verify export integrity (checksums)
4. Encrypt export files (AES-256)

**Phase 3: Transfer (Month 3-4)**
1. Transfer encrypted files to State Archives repository
2. Obtain acknowledgment receipt
3. Verify data integrity at destination

**Phase 4: Anonymization (Month 4-5)**
1. Remove personal identifiers from original database:
   - Replace `owner_name` with pseudonym
   - Replace `owner_id_number` with hash
   - Replace `owner_phone` with NULL
2. Retain non-personal data:
   - GPS coordinates (permanent historical record)
   - Land use type
   - Survey date
   - Location identifier (no personal data)
3. Mark records as "Anonymized" with timestamp

**Phase 5: Verification (Month 5-6)**
1. Verify anonymization completed successfully
2. Confirm no personal data remains in anonymized records
3. Update retention tracking table

### 8.3 Secure Deletion Procedures

Per **Decree 13/2023/Nƒê-CP, Article 21** and **NIST SP 800-88 Guidelines**:

## 10. Children's Privacy

### 10.1 Legal Framework

Per **Law on Children 2016, Article 23** and **Decree 13/2023/Nƒê-CP, Article 8**, special protections apply to children's personal data.

**Child Definition:** Person under 16 years old (Law on Children 2016)

### 10.2 Data Collection Involving Children

#### Scenarios Where Children's Data May Be Collected:

1. **Child is Property Owner:** Rare (typically via inheritance)
   - **Parental Consent Required:** Yes (Law on Children 2016, Article 23)
   - **Guardian Information:** Guardian name and ID must be recorded
   - **Implementation:** Additional field in survey form for guardian information

2. **Child in Photos:** Child visible in property photos
   - **Consent Required:** Parental consent recommended but not strictly required for cadastral purposes (public interest)
   - **Mitigation:** Officer training to avoid focusing camera on children
   - **Future Enhancement:** Face blurring for minors

3. **Child as Survey Respondent:** Child provides information about property
   - **Validity:** Information from children under 16 is NOT valid for cadastral purposes
   - **Requirement:** Adult owner or guardian must be present
   - **Officer Training:** Never accept information from unaccompanied minors

### 10.3 Parental Consent Procedures

When child's personal data must be collected:

**Step 1: Verify Child Status**
- Request ID number of property owner
- If under 16 years old, proceed to Step 2

**Step 2: Identify Guardian**
- Request guardian's full name and ID number
- Verify guardian relationship (birth certificate, court order)

**Step 3: Obtain Consent**
- Explain data collection to guardian
- Obtain verbal consent (recorded in survey notes)
- For sensitive surveys, obtain written consent (future enhancement)

**Step 4: Document**
- Record guardian information in survey
- Note "Parental consent obtained" in survey notes field

### 10.4 Children's Right to Erasure

Per **Law on Children 2016, Article 23.3**, children (or their guardians) have enhanced right to erasure:

- **No 10-year retention requirement:** Children's data can be deleted upon request
- **Guardian Request:** Guardian can request deletion at any time
- **Processing Time:** Within 15 days (faster than standard 30 days)
- **Exceptions:** Still subject to legal obligations (cadastral registration may require retention)

## 11. Data Breach Response

### 11.1 Data Breach Definition

Per **Decree 13/2023/Nƒê-CP, Article 14.1**, a data breach is:

> "Unauthorized or unlawful access, use, disclosure, alteration, or destruction of personal data, or accidental loss of access to personal data."

#### Breach Categories

| Category | Examples | Severity |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **Confidentiality Breach** | Unauthorized access to database, stolen device, data leak | High |
| **Integrity Breach** | Unauthorized modification of survey data, data corruption | Medium |
| **Availability Breach** | Ransomware, DDoS attack, data deletion | Medium |

### 11.2 Breach Notification Requirements

Per **Decree 13/2023/Nƒê-CP, Article 14.2-14.3**:

#### Notification to Authority (Ministry of Public Security)
- **Deadline:** Within **72 hours** of breach discovery
- **Method:** Official written notification (vƒÉn b·∫£n)
- **Content:**
  1. Nature of the breach
  2. Categories of data affected
  3. Number of affected data subjects
  4. Likely consequences
  5. Measures taken to address the breach
  6. Contact person for more information

#### Notification to Data Subjects (Property Owners)
- **Condition:** When breach likely results in **high risk** to rights and freedoms
- **Deadline:** Without undue delay after breach discovery
- **Method:** Direct communication (phone, SMS, or in-person)
- **Content:**
  1. Nature of the breach in clear and plain language
  2. Contact person for more information
  3. Recommended measures to mitigate harm (e.g., monitor credit, change passwords)

**High Risk Assessment:**
| Breach Type | High Risk? | Notification Required? |
|-------------|-----------|----------------------|
| Unauthorized access to 1-10 records | ‚ùå No | Notify authority, not individuals |
| Unauthorized access to 100+ records | ‚úÖ Yes | Notify authority AND individuals |
| Data publicly disclosed online | ‚úÖ Yes | Notify authority AND individuals |
| Sensitive data (ID numbers) compromised | ‚úÖ Yes | Notify authority AND individuals |
| Data encrypted and key not compromised | ‚ùå No | Notify authority, not individuals |

### 11.3 Breach Response Procedures

#### Phase 1: Detection and Containment (0-4 hours)

**1.1 Breach Detection Channels:**
- Security monitoring alerts (Supabase)
- User reports (officer or citizen complaint)
- Audit log analysis
- Third-party notification (e.g., security researcher)

**1.2 Initial Assessment:**
- Confirm breach occurred (not false positive)
- Identify breach type (confidentiality/integrity/availability)
- Estimate scope (number of affected records)

**1.3 Immediate Containment:**
- **Compromised Account:** Revoke access immediately, force password reset
- **Database Breach:** Rotate database credentials, review firewall rules
- **Stolen Device:** Initiate remote wipe if MDM available
- **Malware:** Isolate affected systems, run anti-malware scan

**1.4 Assign Incident Commander:**
- **Role:** Project Manager or Compliance Officer
- **Responsibilities:** Coordinate response, communicate with stakeholders, document incident
